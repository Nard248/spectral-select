\begin{IEEEbiography}[\meloyanphoto]{Narek Meloyan}
received his B.S. degree in Data Science from the American University of Armenia (AUA), where he is currently pursuing an M.S. in Computer and Information Science and serves as a Teaching Associate. He is a Software Architect and Tech Lead at Wireless2020 and a researcher at the YSU Photonics AI Research Group and the L.A. Orbeli Institute of Physiology. His main research interests include deep learning and its applications in scientific domains, particularly medicine and biomedical engineering.
\end{IEEEbiography}

\vspace{-10.5cm}

\begin{IEEEbiography}[\sarvazyanphoto]{Narine Sarvazyan}
earned her B.Sc. in physics (1983) and M.Sc. in biophysics (1986) from Moscow State University, followed by a Ph.D. from the National Armenian Academy of Sciences (1991). After completing postdoctoral fellowships at the Belgrade Institute of General Physical Chemistry and the Medical College of Ohio (1991--1994), and following ten years on the faculty at Texas Tech University, she joined George Washington University, where she is currently a tenured Professor of Pharmacology and Physiology. Her international leadership roles include the William Fraser Endowed Professorship at the American University of Armenia and the ERA Chair award from the European Union. Dr. Sarvazyan's research program focuses on the mechanisms of cardiac arrhythmias, cardiotoxicity, and hyperspectral imaging for visualization of biomedical targets. Her honors include the American Heart Association Established Investigator Award, the U.S. Fulbright Scholar Award, and Armenia's Presidential Medal of Gratitude.
\end{IEEEbiography}
